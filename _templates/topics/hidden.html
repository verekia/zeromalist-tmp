Hidden Stuff Zero

new10-12-2015

Pros: Containers are your enemy. Boxes, drawers, closets, hidden spaces like under your bed, trick you into thinking your mess is tidy by hiding it. I prefer keeping most things I own somewhat visible for a better control. It forces me to reconsider getting rid of unused junk more often.

Feature unlocked: Since I barely use storage furniture, I don't need to buy much of them, so it saves money and space. Also, being able to estimate the volume of things you own in a glimpse makes it easier to plan when moving. No surprises like a forgotten big box in the closet.

How to: The general rule of thumb is to avoid putting things in places that are difficult to see. Avoid the highest shelves that require a chair to reach, or putting items behind others, making them invisible and forgettable. If you thoroughly decluttered your home, you shouldn't have much left, so the visual pollution should be pretty minimal.

Bonus: An extension to this approach of preferring content over containers can also be applied to consumable goods wrappings. I'd rather see 3 folded tissues than the ugly wrinkled plastic wrapping around them. Same goes for food that is wrapped in plastic, itself wrapped in a cardboard box. Getting rid of the outer box gives you a better vision of the food left inside.
